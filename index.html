Examining NBA Data

As an avid watcher of Inside the NBA, one of the things that has always interested me is the pundits' takes on how "the others" perform. Is it really true, for instance, that role players play better at home?

After all, coaching decisions and team strategies are reliant on the perceptions of how certain roles are expected to perform in different situations. For example, coaches often pull their star players at key points when they pick up their 3rd, 4th and 5th fouls too early into the game. There are several motivations behind this: time on the bench can help focus a player frustrated at a call, refs tighten up and blow the whistle less in the final minutes of close games, and of course, there is the conventional wisdom that star players will make or break the games in clutch situations. But is this really true? This project is meant to be a casual attempt to divine some insights here.
